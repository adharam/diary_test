# Conventions

This document fixes the notation and sign conventions used in this repository.

The main goal of the repository is to reproduce and explore Komarov's
Kowalewski-type basis for the hydrogen atom / Kepler problem by symbolic and
finite-dimensional computations.

We distinguish carefully between:

1. the physical Kepler Hamiltonian,
2. the dynamical symmetry algebra generated by angular momentum and the
   Runge--Lenz vector,
3. Komarov's auxiliary Kowalewski-type Hamiltonian inside that algebra.

Throughout the repository, formulas are written in KaTeX-compatible Markdown.

---

## 1. Units for the Kepler / hydrogen problem

We use atomic units unless otherwise stated:

$$
\hbar = 1,\qquad m = 1,\qquad \kappa = 1.
$$

The classical Kepler Hamiltonian is normalized as

$$
H_{\rm{Kep}}(q,p)
=
\frac{1}{2}|p|^2-\frac{1}{r},
\qquad
r=|q|.
$$

The quantum hydrogen Hamiltonian is

$$
\widehat H_{\mathrm Kep}
=
-\frac{1}{2}\Delta-\frac{1}{r}.
$$

The bound-state energies are

$$
E_n=-\frac{1}{2n^2},
\qquad
n=1,2,3,\dots.
$$

In the code, the physical Kepler Hamiltonian should be named

```python
H_kep
```

or

```python
H_coulomb
```

not simply `H`, because `H` is reserved in several files for Komarov's
Kowalewski-type algebraic Hamiltonian.

---

## 2. Physical angular momentum and Runge--Lenz vector

Classically,

$$
L^{\mathrm phys}=q\times p,
$$

and the Runge--Lenz vector is

$$
M^{\mathrm phys}
=
p\times L^{\mathrm phys}-\frac{q}{r}.
$$

It satisfies the usual canonical Poisson-bracket relations

$$
\{L^{\mathrm phys}_i,L^{\mathrm phys}_j\}
=
\varepsilon_{ijk}L^{\mathrm phys}_k,
$$

$$
\{L^{\mathrm phys}_i,M^{\mathrm phys}_j\}
=
\varepsilon_{ijk}M^{\mathrm phys}_k,
$$

$$
\{M^{\mathrm phys}_i,M^{\mathrm phys}_j\}
=
-2H_{\mathrm Kep}\,\varepsilon_{ijk}L^{\mathrm phys}_k.
$$

Quantum mechanically,

$$
\widehat L^{\mathrm phys}
=
\widehat q\times \widehat p,
\qquad
\widehat p=-i\nabla,
$$

and we use the symmetrized Runge--Lenz vector

$$
\widehat M^{\mathrm phys}
=
\frac{1}{2}
\left(
\widehat p\times \widehat L^{\mathrm phys}
-
\widehat L^{\mathrm phys}\times \widehat p
\right)
-
\frac{\widehat q}{r}.
$$

## 3. Komarov convention for the abstract algebra

Komarov uses a left-action convention for the generators. In this repository,
unless explicitly stated otherwise, the abstract generators

$$
L_i,\qquad A_i
$$

satisfy the Komarov-sign convention.

The classical bracket used in the algebra modules is

$$
[F,G]_{\mathrm cl}:=-\{F,G\}_{\mathrm canonical}.
$$

Thus

$$
[L_i,L_j]_{\mathrm cl}
=
-\varepsilon_{ijk}L_k,
$$

$$
[L_i,A_j]_{\mathrm cl}
=
-\varepsilon_{ijk}A_k,
$$

$$
[A_i,A_j]_{\mathrm cl}
=
\mathcal P\,\varepsilon_{ijk}L_k.
$$

The parameter

$$
\mathcal P\in\{-1,0,+1\}
$$

labels the dynamical symmetry algebra:

| Kepler energy | Algebra | Komarov parameter |
|---|---:|---:|
| \(E<0\) | \(o(4)\) | \(\mathcal P=-1\) |
| \(E=0\) | \(e(3)\) | \(\mathcal P=0\) |
| \(E>0\) | \(o(3,1)\) | \(\mathcal P=+1\) |

On a fixed nonzero energy shell, the scaled Runge--Lenz generator is

$$
A_i=
\begin{cases}
\dfrac{M^{\mathrm phys}_i}{\sqrt{-2E}}, & E<0,\\[1.2em]
\dfrac{M^{\mathrm phys}_i}{\sqrt{2E}}, & E>0.
\end{cases}
$$

For \(E=0\), use \(A_i=M^{\mathrm phys}_i\).

## 4. Quantum commutator convention

The quantum algebra in the repository is the abstract Komarov algebra

$$
[L_i,L_j]
=
-i\varepsilon_{ijk}L_k,
$$

$$
[L_i,A_j]
=
-i\varepsilon_{ijk}A_k,
$$

$$
[A_i,A_j]
=
i\mathcal P\,\varepsilon_{ijk}L_k.
$$

This is not the same sign convention as the most common physical convention
for the coordinate-space angular momentum operator. To realize it from the
usual physical operators, one may set schematically

$$
L_i=-L^{\mathrm phys}_i,
\qquad
A_i=-A^{\mathrm phys}_i.
$$

However, most symbolic computations in this repository should work directly
with the abstract relations above.

---

## 5. Index and epsilon conventions

We use

$$
\varepsilon_{123}=+1.
$$

Repeated indices are summed only when explicitly stated. In code, prefer
explicit sums over implicit Einstein notation.

The square norms are

$$
L^2=L_1^2+L_2^2+L_3^2,
\qquad
A^2=A_1^2+A_2^2+A_3^2.
$$

---

## 6. Ladder variables

We define

$$
L_\pm=L_1\pm iL_2,
\qquad
A_\pm=A_1\pm iA_2.
$$

In the quantum algebra,

$$
L_\pm^\dagger=L_\mp,
\qquad
A_\pm^\dagger=A_\mp.
$$

For products,

$$
(XY)^\dagger=Y^\dagger X^\dagger.
$$

The anticommutator is

$$
\{X,Y\}=XY+YX.
$$

---

## 7. Casimir operators

For the unified Komarov algebra, the two Casimir operators are

$$
C_1=A^2-\mathcal P L^2,
$$

and

$$
C_2=A\cdot L=L\cdot A.
$$

Komarov often writes

$$
C_1=a^2,
\qquad
C_2=la.
$$

For the hydrogen bound states, where \(\mathcal P=-1\), one has

$$
C_1=A^2+L^2.
$$

With the standard atomic normalization,

$$
E_n=-\frac{1}{2n^2},
$$

and, in the physical hydrogen representation,

$$
C_1=n^2-1,
\qquad
C_2=0.
$$

Equivalently, the \(o(4)\simeq su(2)\oplus su(2)\) representation is

$$
j_+=j_-=\frac{n-1}{2}.
$$

## 8. Name collision: physical Hamiltonian vs Komarov Hamiltonian

The symbol \(H\) is overloaded in the literature.

In this repository:

| Meaning | Recommended symbol | Code name |
|---|---|---|
| Physical Kepler Hamiltonian | \(H_{\mathrm Kep}\) | `H_kep` |
| Quantum hydrogen Hamiltonian | \(\widehat H_{\mathrm Kep}\) | `H_hydrogen` |
| Komarov Kowalewski-type Hamiltonian | \(\mathsf H\) | `H_kom` |
| Modified Komarov Hamiltonians | \(\mathsf H_{(+)},\mathsf H_{(-)}\) | `H_plus`, `H_minus` |

Komarov's algebraic Hamiltonian is

$$
\mathsf H=L^2+L_3^2-A_1.
$$

This \(\mathsf H\) is not the physical Kepler Hamiltonian. It is an additional
operator inside the dynamical symmetry algebra, used to define a Kowalewski-type
basis within a fixed Kepler energy sector.

---

## 9. Classical vs quantum products

In the classical algebra:

- products commute,
- ordering is ignored,
- \(k_+k_-=k_-k_+\).

In the quantum algebra:

- products do not commute,
- ordering is part of the data,
- \(k_+k_-\) and \(k_-k_+\) must be distinguished,
- self-adjointness must be checked using the dagger operation.

This is the main reason the quantum theory has several inequivalent
Kowalewski-type analogues while the classical theory has a single expression.

## 10. Implementation policy

Symbolic code should keep the following modules conceptually separate:

```text
algebra/
  commutators.py        # abstract quantum commutators
  lie_poisson.py        # classical Lie-Poisson bracket
  normal_order.py       # noncommutative simplification rules

classical/
  komarov_classical.py  # classical H, k_±, K_cl

quantum/
  komarov_quantum.py    # quantum H, K, H_±, K_±
```

Tests should explicitly specify whether they are classical or quantum:

```text
test_classical_kowalewski.py
test_quantum_kowalewski.py
```

Do not reuse a classical simplification routine inside the quantum module unless
it is explicitly taking a classical limit.
